Profile

Hi, I'm Luke Tait. I am a mathematician and neuroscientist at Cardiff University. The focus of my research involves applying methods from mathematics and physics to brain imaging data, to better understand healthy cognition and mechanisms underpinning neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's disease. Particularly, I am interested in large scale functional connectivity (i.e. synchronous activity) between regions of the brain. I graduated with a Masters degree in Mathematical Physics from the University of Liverpool in 2015, and subsequently did a PhD in Mathematics at the University of Exeter Living Systems Institute. I joined the Cardiff University Brain Research Imaging Centre in July 2019 as a research associate in the Cognition and Computational Brain Lab.
